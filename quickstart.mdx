## Before you start

<Callout kind="info">

WireCare is a web application used by wildlife rehabilitation organisations to track animals, volunteers, tasks, and finance. Access it in a modern desktop browser such as Chrome, Firefox, or Safari.

</Callout>

You need:

- A work or personal email address you can access
- Your name and mobile number for contact records
- An invitation or confirmation from your organisation about which role you will use (for example, volunteer, coordinator, treasurer, admin)

If your team has not configured WireCare yet, share the [Introduction](/introduction) and [Configuration](/configuration) pages with your local administrator.

## Create your account

New staff and volunteers create their own login using the built-in registration page.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the register page" icon="log-in">
    Go to the `/register` route in your organisation's WireCare deployment. Your coordinator may send this as a link.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your details" icon="users">
    Fill in the registration form:

    - **Full name** for identification on rosters and task lists  
    - **Email address** used for sign-in and notifications  
    - **Password** that meets your organisation's security rules  
    - **Phone number** so coordinators can contact you about shifts and animals

  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit and check your inbox" icon="mail">
    Submit the form. WireCare uses Supabase authentication and sends a verification email to the address you entered.
    
    Open the email and select the verification link to confirm your account.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to WireCare" icon="layout-dashboard">
    After you select the verification link, your browser returns you to WireCare.

    If the verification opened in a different browser or device, go back to the original browser window so you stay in the same session.

  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="alert">

WireCare requires a confirmed email. Until you verify your email, you will see limited access and an **Email Verification Required** banner on the dashboard.

</Callout>

## Log in and reach the dashboard

Once your email is confirmed, sign in and confirm that you can see the main modules.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the login page" icon="log-in">
    Go to `/login`. The root route `/` also redirects to the login page.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in" icon="users">
    Enter the same email and password you used during registration, then submit.

    If the credentials are valid, you move into the authenticated part of the app and the navigation bar appears at the top.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm email status" icon="mail">
    If your email is not yet confirmed, the dashboard shows an **Email Verification Required** banner.

    Open the latest verification email from WireCare, select the link, then refresh the dashboard.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate the main navigation" icon="layout-dashboard">
    After confirmation, the navbar shows core modules such as:

    - **Dashboard**  
    - **Animals**  
    - **Roster**  
    - **Shift Tasks** (animal care tasks)  
    - **Foliage Map**  
    - **Possum Helper**  
    - **Resources**

    Depending on your role, you may also see **Finance** or **Admin Panel** sections.

  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">

If you cannot access the dashboard after login, review the dedicated guide in the Help Center: [Cannot Access Dashboard](/help-center/troubleshooting/cannot-access-dashboard).

</Callout>

## Role-based first steps

Different roles use WireCare differently on day one. Follow the tab that best matches your role.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Volunteer / Carer" icon="users">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Confirm you can see animals and roster" icon="layout-dashboard">
        Once logged in, check that the navbar includes **Animals**, **Roster**, **Shift Tasks**, and **Resources**.

        If you see an empty dashboard with no animals or rosters, your account may not be linked to a branch yet.

      </Step>
      <Step title="Review your current animals" icon="paw-print">
        Select **Animals** to open the `/animals` route.

        Use this list to see animals currently assigned to your branch.

      </Step>
      <Step title="Check your roster and tasks" icon="clipboard-check">
        Select **Roster** to open `/roster` and see current or upcoming assignments.

        Then open **Shift Tasks** or **Animal Care Tasks** at `/animal-care-tasks` to view daily care work, such as feeding or medication lists.

      </Step>
      <Step title="Access resources" icon="book-open">
        Select **Resources** to open `/resources`. Your organisation stores care protocols, contact trees, and other reference material here.

        Keep this open in a separate tab if you need to refer to procedures while working.

      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Coordinator" icon="calendar">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Check branch animals" icon="paw-print">
        Open **Animals** (`/animals`) to confirm that animals for your branch load correctly.

        If you do not see the expected animals, your user may not be assigned to the right branch. Ask an Admin to confirm your branch and role mapping.

      </Step>
      <Step title="Add a test animal" icon="square-check">
        Select **Animals** then use the add route `/animals/add` to create a new record.

        Enter a realistic test animal for your branch so you can practise intake and task assignment without affecting live data.

      </Step>
      <Step title="Review future intakes" icon="layout-dashboard">
        Open **Future Intakes** at `/future-intakes` to see incoming animals that have been flagged in advance.

        Use this to plan capacity, roster coverage, and cage availability.

      </Step>
      <Step title="Review roster and tasks" icon="clipboard-check">
        Open `/roster` to see volunteer coverage, then `/animal-care-tasks` to review current task distribution.

        Use these views together when planning who will take which animals or shifts.

      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Treasurer" icon="dollar-sign">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Confirm Finance access" icon="shield">
        After login, look for the **Finance** or **Treasurer** item in the navbar, which routes to `/finance`.

        If you cannot see it, your account may not have the Treasurer role yet. Ask an Admin to adjust your role.

      </Step>
      <Step title="Open Finance module" icon="dollar-sign">
        Select **Finance** to open `/finance`.

        Review the layout and confirm that any existing financial records for your organisation appear as expected.

      </Step>
      <Step title="Coordinate with Admin" icon="users">
        Work with your Admin to confirm which users should have access to finance data and how often records are updated.

        Use this quickstart as a reference for ensuring the right people have the right level of access.

      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Admin / System Admin" icon="shield">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the Admin Panel" icon="layout-dashboard">
        After login, check for the **Admin Panel** entry in the navbar and open `/admin-panel`.

        From here you manage user accounts, roles, and branches.

      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify your own role and branch" icon="users">
        In the Admin tools, confirm that your user has an administrator role and is assigned to the correct branch or organisation-wide scope.

        This ensures you can see all the animals, rosters, and configuration you expect.

      </Step>
      <Step title="Check that each key role exists" icon="clipboard-check">
        Review user roles to ensure that you have at least one active:

        - **Coordinator** for each branch  
        - **Treasurer** if you use the Finance module  
        - **Admin** or system admin for configuration and user management

        Assign roles and branches where required so staff and volunteers see the correct data.

      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Callout kind="alert">

If a user can log in and see the navbar but no animals, rosters, or branch data, their account is likely missing a role or branch assignment. Ask an Admin to assign an appropriate role and branch in the Admin Panel.

</Callout>

## Record your first animal

Once you can see the **Animals** module and have appropriate permissions, add a real or test animal to confirm the workflow.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Animals list" icon="paw-print">
    From the navbar, select **Animals** to open `/animals`.

    Confirm that existing animals (if any) appear with the expected branch context.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new record" icon="square-check">
    From the Animals view, use the **Add** option to navigate to `/animals/add`.

    WireCare shows the animal intake form for your branch.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill out key details" icon="clipboard-check">
    Enter core information about the animal, such as species, intake date, location found, and any immediate health notes.

    Use realistic details that make sense for your branch so staff can recognise the case later.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and confirm visibility" icon="layout-dashboard">
    Save the record and return to the `/animals` list.

    Confirm that the new animal appears and that other users with the same branch and appropriate role can see it.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## Join the roster and tasks

After confirming animals, ensure your roster and care tasks views are working.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the roster" icon="calendar">
    Select **Roster** in the navbar to open `/roster`.

    Review existing and upcoming shifts. Coordinators use this to plan coverage.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Review animal care tasks" icon="clipboard-check">
    Navigate to `/animal-care-tasks` via **Shift Tasks** or **Animal Care Tasks** in the navbar.

    Confirm that tasks linked to current animals appear as expected and that you can see tasks relevant to your branch.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Check future work" icon="layout-dashboard">
    Open `/future-intakes` to review planned animal arrivals and any associated planning tasks.

    Use this information with the roster to identify gaps in coverage.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## Explore maps and resources

Maps and helper tools provide extra context for field work and habitat decisions.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Foliage Map" icon="map-pin">
    Select **Foliage Map** in the navbar to open `/foliage-map`.

    Use this view to explore relevant vegetation or habitat information around your branch area.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Use Possum Helper" icon="map-pin">
    Navigate to `/possum-helper`.

    This helper supports possum-related decisions such as suitable release areas and care considerations defined by your organisation.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse Resources" icon="book-open">
    Open `/resources` via the **Resources** link in the navbar.

    Check that your organisation's documents, policies, and other references are available and accessible to the right roles.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## Finance and Admin access

Finance and administration tools are restricted to specific roles.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check if you see Finance" icon="dollar-sign">
    Look for **Finance** in the navbar. If it is visible, open `/finance` to confirm the module loads.

    If you are a Treasurer and cannot see it, contact an Admin to update your role.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Admin Panel visibility" icon="shield">
    If you are a system administrator, ensure that the **Admin Panel** entry is visible and opens `/admin-panel`.

    From there, review users, roles, and branches to confirm your organisation's structure is correct.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Resolve access gaps" icon="users">
    When someone reports that they cannot see animals, roster entries, or finance data:

    - Check their **role** (volunteer, coordinator, treasurer, admin)  
    - Check their **branch** assignment  
    - Adjust these in the Admin Panel, then ask them to log out and back in

  </Step>
</Steps>
